The Opportunity

A River Runs Through It

The Genesee River has shaped Rochester's history. Now it can shape our future. At 200 feet wide and 13 feet deep, our river is a natural transit corridor waiting to be activated.

Water Transit

Modern water taxis traveling at 25 knots, connecting RIT to High Falls in under 20 minutes. Clean, quiet, and scenic transportation along our historic river.

Aerial Gondola

An elegant gondola lift bypassing the waterfalls, connecting High Falls to the northern reaches. A new perspective on Rochester's most dramatic natural feature.

Connected Destinations

From RIT to Ontario Beach, access Rochester's universities, hospitals, parks, breweries, sports venues, and cultural institutions along one unified corridor.

Why Now

Rochester Deserves Better Transit

What do RIT, U of R, the Blue Cross Arena, Seneca Park Zoo, Genesee Brew House, and Strong Museum have in common?

They all rest on or near the banks of the Genesee River. A successful implementation of the GRT would connect Rochester's most important institutions and restore urban vibrancy by making the heart of the city accessible to young professionals, students, families, and visitors alike.

The river is already there. It's an average of 200 feet across and 13 feet deep—plenty of space for bidirectional transit. This plan requires minimal new infrastructure compared to light rail, making it remarkably achievable.

The Plan

Three Phases to Transform Transit

A modular approach that can scale to serve 10,000 rides per day, rivaling major metro systems.

01
Water Transit

RIT to High Falls

Launch water taxi service along the southern Genesee, connecting RIT, U of R, and downtown Rochester. The foundation of the entire system.

02
Aerial Gondola

Over the Falls

A 2.5-mile gondola lift connecting High Falls to Low Falls, bypassing the waterfalls with a spectacular aerial experience.

03
Lake Connection

To Charlotte & Beyond

Extend water service north to Charlotte and Ontario Beach Park, completing Rochester's north-south transit backbone.
