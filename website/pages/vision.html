The Vision

A River Renewed

"What do these places have in common? They all rest on or near the banks of the Genesee River."

The Opportunity Before Us

The airport. RIT. The University of Rochester. The Blue Cross Arena. Seneca Park Zoo. Genesee Brew House. The Strong Museum. Strong Hospital. What do these places have in common?

They all rest on or near the banks of the Genesee River.

Rochester was built on the Genesee. The river powered our flour mills, drove our industry, and shaped the geography of our neighborhoods. Yet today, this remarkable natural corridor sits largely underutilized—a gap in our city's connective tissue.

The Genesee River Transit proposal aims to change that. By implementing modern water taxis along the river and an aerial gondola to bypass the waterfalls, we can create an efficient, cost-effective, and beautiful transit system that finally connects our city's most important institutions while restoring urban vibrancy to Rochester.

Natural Infrastructure

The River Is Already There

The beautiful thing about the Genesee River is that it already exists. It averages 200 feet across and 13 feet deep—plenty of space for bidirectional transit operations.

Unlike light rail or subway systems that require billions in infrastructure investment, water transit leverages existing geography. The construction costs are dramatically lower, the timeline is faster, and the system can be scaled incrementally.

At 25 knots service speed, a water taxi can travel from RIT to High Falls in under 20 minutes without stops. That's competitive with driving during off-peak hours—and significantly faster during rush hour.

Multi-Modal Transit

Water. Air. Connection.

The GRT combines two complementary transit modes to overcome Rochester's unique geography.

Water Taxis

Modern, electric-hybrid ferries operating on the navigable stretches of the Genesee River. Fast, quiet, and offering passengers a unique perspective on Rochester from the water.

  • + RIT to downtown in under 20 minutes
  • + Maximum 6-minute wait times at peak
  • + Year-round operation with ice-capable vessels

Aerial Gondola

A 2.5-mile gondola lift connecting High Falls to the northern reaches, bypassing the dramatic waterfalls while offering spectacular views of Rochester's most iconic natural feature.

  • + Connects the two navigable river sections
  • + Views of High Falls and gorge
  • + Weather-protected cabins

Unlocking Potential

Connecting Students to the City

Rochester is home to world-class universities, yet our students remain largely disconnected from downtown and the cultural life of the city. RIT and U of R students often spend four years here without ever truly experiencing Rochester.

The GRT would change that equation entirely. With quick, reliable transit from campus to downtown, students could live in the city center and commute to class—or easily head downtown for dinner, a concert, or a ball game.

"Finally enabling RIT and U of R students to live and engage with our historic downtown."

Why Isn't This Already Built?

The main question faced when researching this proposal is: "Why isn't this already implemented?" Is there some fatal flaw that's been missed?

The unit economics make sense. The demand is clearly there—anyone who has driven in Rochester knows the frustration of our car-centric infrastructure. And unlike light rail, barely any new infrastructure would need to be built.

As far as our research goes, there isn't a downside. There's only opportunity.
